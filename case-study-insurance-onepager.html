Client Situation

The insurer already had claims software, CRM, policy database, email and WhatsApp channels, plus document folders and approval rules. Teams still lost time handling fragmented inputs.

  • Scattered claim evidence across messages and attachments
  • Inconsistent responses from agents
  • Repeated SOP questions from new hires
  • Limited visibility into delay root causes

ZAD Layer Added

ZAD sat above existing systems and structured work signals in real time, while keeping the claims platform as the system of record.

  • AI intake extracts and routes claim essentials
  • Agent assistant drafts policy-aligned responses
  • In-workflow coaching for new employees
  • Manager cockpit surfaces SLA and document risks

Operational Signal Found

Repair estimates were the dominant delay driver in auto claims. Two partner garages showed materially slower response patterns than peers.

  • Garage A average estimate delay: 3.8 days
  • Garage B average estimate delay: 2.9 days
  • Garage C average estimate delay: 0.9 days

Recommended Action

Create a fast-estimate lane: auto-reminders after 24 hours, SLA escalation for slow partners, and urgent routing toward faster estimate providers.

  • Faster claims resolution cycle
  • Improved customer communication quality
  • Better partner management decisions
Strategic value: The AI layer does not just process claims faster. It reveals operational patterns that leadership can use to reduce delay and improve service outcomes.
